Steve Jobs

Visionary Entrepreneur and Technologist

Here is a timeline of Job's life

  • 1955 - Born in San Francisco, California, to Abdulfattah Jandali and Joanne Schieble. He is later adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs.
  • 1976 - Co-founded Apple Computer, Inc. with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne in the garage of his parents' home. They release the Apple I computer.
  • 1984 - Launches the Macintosh, a revolutionary personal computer with a graphical user interface and mouse, which becomes a commercial success.
  • 1985 - Forced out of Apple after a power struggle with the board of directors. Jobs starts NeXT Inc., a computer company that focuses on higher education and business markets.
  • 1986 - Acquires Pixar Animation Studios from George Lucas and becomes the majority shareholder. Under his leadership, Pixar produces successful animated films like Toy Story, Finding Nemo, and The Incredibles.
  • 1996 - Apple acquires NeXT Inc., bringing Steve Jobs back to the company. He becomes the interim CEO and later takes the role permanently.
  • 1998 - Launches the iMac, a colorful all-in-one computer, which revitalizes Apple's product line and begins the company's resurgence.
  • 2001 - Introduces the iPod, a portable digital music player that revolutionizes the music industry and paves the way for the iTunes Store.
  • 2007 - Unveils the iPhone, a touchscreen smartphone that changes the mobile industry and leads to the widespread adoption of smartphones worldwide.
  • 2011 - Announces the iPad, a tablet computer, which creates a new product category and becomes immensely popular, further solidifying Apple's position as a technology leader.
  • 2011 - Steve Jobs passes away on October 5, leaving behind a profound legacy as an entrepreneur, visionary, and one of the most influential figures in the technology industry.

"Steve was among the greatest of American innovators - brave enough to think differently, bold enough to believe he could change the world, and talented enough to do it."

-- Barrack Obama, former President of the United States
